How the math works & why these guardrails

Gross margin = profit as a % of the price you charge. Price = True Cost ÷ (1 − margin). A 45% margin means cost is 55% of price.
Markup is the % added on top of cost — a 45% margin equals an ~82% markup. The tool shows both so you don't confuse them.
True job cost = subs + materials (+ tax) + equipment + permits + disposal + your labor, then a contingency buffer for the unknowns.
Break-even covers your true cost plus allocated overhead — quote below it and the job loses money even if it "feels" profitable.
Net profit is what's left after overhead. A 40–50% gross margin on subcontracted work typically nets ~28–38% after a 12% overhead load — that's your real wiggle room.

Reality check on the target: most LA general contractors mark up subcontracted/pass-through work 20–35% (≈17–26% margin). A 40–50% gross-margin target is healthy for self-performed trade work (painting, drywall, handyman) and gives negotiating room, but on large pass-through GC jobs the over-pricing flag will warn you when the market won't bear it. Don't lose the bid chasing the number.

LA 2026 benchmarks built in — painting $3–$6.50/sf · drywall labor $1–$3/sf · flooring $2.50–$18/sf installed (by material) · dry-room remodel ~$100+/sf · bath remodel $200–$600/sf · kitchen remodel $135–$450+/sf · MEP service labor $95–$200/hr. Always your judgment over the calculator.
